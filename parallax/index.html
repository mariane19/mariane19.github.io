The Origins of Jordan Brand

Jordan Brand is a signature shoe and athletic wear brand owned and created by the legendary basketball player Michael Jordan. Nike first signed Jordan under an endorsement deal in 1984 and subsequently launched the Air Jordan line of basketball shoes.

The Early Success of Air Jordan

In his first NBA season, Michael Jordan averaged 28.2 points per game while wearing the original Air Jordan I, earning him the Rookie of the Year award. This early success and popularity of the Air Jordan line helped establish the brand and generate significant buzz around Jordan and his signature shoes.

The Iconic Air Jordan Designs

Over the course of Michael Jordan's illustrious career, the Jordan Brand continued to innovate and release iconic Air Jordan designs. Notable examples include the Air Jordan III, which featured the Jumpman logo and visible Nike Air bubble, and the Air Jordan XI, known for its patent leather sheen. These designs were inspired by Jordan's athletic prowess and helped cement the brand's status as a cultural icon.

Expanding Beyond Basketball

After Michael Jordan's retirement, the Jordan Brand expanded beyond basketball by signing athletes and celebrities from various disciplines, including Derek Jeter, Ray Allen, Randy Moss, Drake, Russell Westbrook, and Kawhi Leonard. This broadened the brand's reach and appeal, establishing the Jordan name as a symbol of greatness across multiple sports and industries.

The Future of Jordan Brand

​The Jordan Brand is committed to pushing the limits, redefining what's possible, and continuing to achieve greatness, even after Michael Jordan's retirement.The brand's focus on innovation and its ability to adapt to new generations of athletes and consumers ensures that the legacy of Air Jordan will live on for years to come.
